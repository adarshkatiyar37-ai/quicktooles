What is Base64 Encoding?

Base64 encoding is a standardized procedure that transforms complex data into a safe, universally readable string of text. The "64" refers to the specific characters: A-Z, a-z, 0-9, (+), and (/).

In the early days of computing, sending data acrossNetworks was risky. Base64 ensures data travels safely without losing structure.

Why Do Developers Use Base64 Encoding?

  • Safe Data Transfer: XML, JSON, or APIs use it to avoid syntax errors.
  • Data URIs: Embedding small images into HTML/CSS files.
  • Email Attachments: Sending files over MIME protocols.

Base64 Encoding vs. Encryption

Encoding (Base64): Used for data usability, not security. Anyone with a decoder can read it.

Encryption (AES/RSA): Scrambles data with a secret key for security.
