What is a Percentage?

A Percentage (represented by the symbol %) is a number or ratio expressed as a fraction of 100. It is derived from the Latin phrase "per centum," which literally means "by the hundred." For example, 45% is simply 45 out of 100, or the fraction $45/100$.

Percentages are used globally to represent probability, interest rates on bank loans, retail discounts, and even human body compositions. Using an online percentage calculator simplifies these complex ratios into easy-to-understand digits.

Calculating Percentage Increase and Decrease

The formula for Percentage Change is one of the most useful in statistics. It is calculated as:

Percentage Change = ((New Value - Original Value) / Original Value) * 100

If the result is positive, it represents an Increase. If the result is negative, it represents a Decrease. Our tool automatically handles the math and displays the exact percentage shift instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q: How do I find 20% of a number?
A: Multiply the number by 20 and then divide by 100. Or simply multiply the number by 0.20 using our basic calculator above.


Q: What is the percentage difference between two numbers?
A: Percentage difference is used when comparing two values of the same type. You divide the absolute difference by the average of the two numbers and multiply by 100.


Q: Can percentages be over 100?
A: Yes! A value of 200% means the amount is twice the original size. In business growth, percentages often exceed 100%.
